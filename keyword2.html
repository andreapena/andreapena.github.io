SPATIALITY : Gretchen Schiller, (2008), proposes a choreomedia; as the organization of a kinesthetic movement qualities, through artistic acts of temporal, spatial and qualitative rapport of people, objects, media and physical space” (2008, 433). I extend Schillers term towards a choreomediation: as the eliciting of actions on the body through the prompting affordances of the built environment. A choreomediation where the affording qualities and functional possibilities of objects, 'dictated’ by designers, proscribes the actions of the individual. Choreography thus as a kinaesthetic inscription is not only embodied in the structural composition of design interactions, yet generated through industrial and homogenizing means.
